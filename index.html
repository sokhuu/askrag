How quickly do I need to report a statutory disqualification?
You need to report a statutory disqualification to FINRA promptly, but in any event not later than 30 calendar days after the member knows or should have known of the existence of the disqualification.
What does Rule 3110 require for branch office inspections?
Rule 3110 requires that each member inspect every branch office at least every three years if the branch office does not supervise one or more non-branch locations. The frequency of inspections should consider the nature and complexity of the securities activities, the volume of business, and the number of associated persons at the location. The member's written supervisory and inspection procedures must outline the examination cycle, the factors used to determine the frequency, and how compliance with the inspection requirements will be maintained.

UPCOMING RULE CHANGE

A rule most firms haven't operationalized yet.

We don't just answer questions about the rulebook as it stands today — here's how we'd flag a change that's already been approved and is about to hit firms' supervisory systems.

FINRA RULE 4210 AMENDMENT
Pattern Day Trader rule replaced with intraday margin standards
SR-FINRA-2025-017 · SEC approved April 14, 2026
Effective June 4, 2026 · Optional phase-in through October 20, 2027

For two decades, firms flagged "pattern day traders" by counting day trades (4 or more within 5 business days) and enforced a flat $25,000 minimum equity requirement on those accounts. That framework is gone. In its place, firms must calculate each margin account's intraday margin deficit — the largest gap between required margin and account equity at any point during the trading day — whenever a trade reduces the account's intraday margin level. Customers get 15 business days to cure a deficit; if it isn't cured by the 5th business day, the firm must freeze further trading in the account for 90 days, with narrow exceptions for small or extraordinary deficits.

HOW THIS HITS AN ACTUAL FIRM
Example: a mid-size introducing broker-dealer

A fictional 38-rep introducing broker-dealer with a retail active-trading desk of roughly 1,150 margin accounts, many currently monitored under the old "pattern day trader" logic.

  • Old workflow, now obsolete: its WSPs and surveillance system flag accounts after 4 day trades in 5 business days and hard-block trading below $25,000 equity — a rule that no longer exists as of June 4, 2026.
  • New workflow, required: its ops team has to stand up intraday margin deficit calculations, choose between real-time or single end-of-day calculation, and build the 15-business-day cure and 90-day freeze process — before June 4, 2026, or by October 20, 2027 if they elect the phase-in.
  • What falls through the cracks without this: if the firm keeps supervising against the old PDT thresholds past the effective date, it's enforcing a rule that's gone — while missing the cure-window and freeze obligations that replaced it.

The rule, filing, and dates above are real (FINRA Rule 4210, SR-FINRA-2025-017). The firm described is fictional, used for illustration — not legal advice.
